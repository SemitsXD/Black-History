Racism to Black Inventors

Racism to African American inventors has always been happening, especially in the 1800s. Many African-Americans were slaves and as slaves, they were not considered as citizens therefore unable to get patents for their invention. For that reason, their owners would usually take credit for their invention(s).

Granville Woods is a African-American inventor who faked being half Indian half Australian so he would not face as much racism.

Other forms of racism to Black inventors include people working in the US Trademark and Pattent Office putting aside a lot of these pattent requests and usually do them a lot later. Black people would also have to go throught a extensive process to get their pattent which was also costly so a lot of black people could not pay for it and it was also hard for them to raise money.

To not face racism and heavy scrutiny, African-American people would tend to either, completely deny the fact that they were black and use other countries out of Africa like India. They could also get a white person to submit the pattent for them or just not get a pattent which did not protect them from copiers.
